When using the Personal Chart and Predictions calculators, there are three calculation methods to choose from:

  • Traditional Nine Star Ki

    Uses a Yang materialising spiral for both men's and women's personal charts.

  • The Chinese Method (Ascending Yin Ki for Females)

    Uses a Yang materialising spiral for men's, and a Yin ascending spiral for women's charts and predictions.

In addition to the two calculation methods, there is an optional flag to determine which method to use for calculating the houses occupied during a given period.

Use Isometric Calculation Method

In traditional 9 Star Ki, the yearly natal Ki is used to determine the houses occupied for periods of all lengths. This method uses isometric period lengths. In other words, it matches year to year, month to month, etc. For example, to determine the 'house' occupied for a given year, the global Ki assigned during a person's birth year is used. This remains unchanged, as the period lengths are isometric. However, when determining monthly houses, rather than tracking the position of the yearly natal Ki number in the Magic Square, the monthly natal Ki is used. This rule applies to all cycle lengths. Proponents of this method argue that the influence of Ki is essentially 'holographic', and the length of the period in question needs to be factored in when determining how the energies combine in their influence over our lives.

Traditional 9 Star Ki

Traditionally, 9 Star Ki and Feng Shui practitioners use the Yang materialising spiral for all calculations, meaning that as each year passes, the Ki descends, cycling from 9 to 1. Proponents of this method argue that it is more appropriate today because we are still in the material age, which affects both men and women alike.

The Chinese Method

This method of calculation originated in China and was believed to be more accurate for women, who are typically more Yin and therefore believed to be more affected by the Yin ascending Ki. This subject is a delicate one and can be a bone of contention in Macrobiotic circles. However, a number of 9 Star Ki practitioners today have also started to use the Yin ascending spiral when calculating charts for female clients and often find that it yields more accurate results. Whilst the Yang descending Ki has a materialising, condensing effect, the Yin ascending Ki is believed to have a more spiritualising and expansive one.

Yin / Yang Ki and Precession

In reality, both Yin and Yang spirals are always present and in effect; however, the Yang materialising spiral is more dominant during the winter season of the Great Year. We are now well into the spring season of the Great Year, and as we move out of Precessional Winter (or 'Kali Yuga', as the yogis like to call it), the influence of the Yin ascending spiral is increasingly being felt — especially by those who are either more sensitive by nature or more spiritually inclined. This is especially noticeable in younger generations.

The Golden Age

As we progress toward the summer season of the Great Year, the influence of the Yin ascending spiral will continue to increase, whilst the Yang materialising spiral will decrease in its effects. By the year 2104, all of humanity will be predominantly affected by the Yin ascending spiral. According to some sources, this is officially the beginning of The Golden Age. However, just as the dawn announces the day long before the sun rises over the horizon, so the transition of the ages is gradual. We are already well into the precessional dawn, and the glow of its aurora is very much felt and seen.

Which method should you use?

We encourage you to use your intuition and to pick the method that best fits your needs. This may well be on a case-by-case basis. At 9starkiastrology.com, we often compare both charts to see which feels intuitively like a better fit. For practitioners, it can be useful to discuss this directly with clients. We have observed that the demographic and age of the person are important factors, as well as how spiritually or metaphysically inclined and sensitive they are. Generally speaking, the younger generations seem to be more influenced by the ascending Ki — which makes sense, as they've been less impacted by the more condensing Yang energies present further into our recent past.
