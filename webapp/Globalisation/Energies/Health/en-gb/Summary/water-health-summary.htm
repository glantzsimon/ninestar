Health and Physical Wellbeing

  • Prone to imbalances in the kidneys, bladder, adrenal glands, reproductive organs, and skeletal system.
  • Cold and damp environments can significantly weaken vitality; warmth is essential, especially around the stomach, lower back, and ears.
  • Women may experience gynecological issues such as irregular menstruation; men may face prostate problems or impotence.
  • Require adequate rest and should avoid late nights, caffeine, alcohol, sugar, and chronic stress.
  • Diet should include good-quality water and natural salt in moderation; avoid heavily salted or extreme-temperature foods.
  • Recommended food approach: macrobiotic or gentle, balanced nourishment.
  • Sensitive to nervous irritation, fear, and emotional rigidity under stress — may express as timidity or paranoia.
  • Stress-related issues can lead to long-term cognitive decline if not addressed.
  • Helpful practices: progressive relaxation, calming music, bilateral stimulation, and rhythmic breathing.
  • Ideal exercise includes yoga, tai chi, swimming in salt or ozone-treated water — focusing on spinal flexibility and fluid movement.
