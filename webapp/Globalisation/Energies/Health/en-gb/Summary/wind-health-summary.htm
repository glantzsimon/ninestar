Health and Wellness
Gifts
  • Sensitive and responsive to the body’s signals and environment.
  • Strong potential for physical vitality, mental clarity, and emotional balance when well cared for.
  • Responsive to meditation, relaxation, and gentle movement practices.
  • Capable of maintaining robust health through steady routines and self-awareness.
Challenges
  • Prone to gall bladder imbalances, digestive weakness, and muscular tension.
  • Stress may lead to fatigue, rigidity, clenched muscles, or arthritic tendencies.
  • Emotional suppression can result in physical symptoms, especially rooted in frustration or anger.
  • Tendency toward overthinking, overplanning, and dietary indulgence (especially flour, dairy, and oily foods).
Supportive Strategies
  • Eat slowly, when hungry, and in a peaceful setting; avoid cold foods and beverages.
  • Engage in regular physical activity—especially walking, jogging, swimming, or cycling.
  • Incorporate meditation, stretching, and light therapy to support nervous system balance and vision.
  • Favor dark leafy greens, calcium-rich foods, and avoid excess sodium, potassium, alcohol, and heavy dairy.
  • Protect the throat and torso from easterly winds, especially in spring; consider massage and isometric exercise.
