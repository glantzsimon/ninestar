Gifts
  • Introspective and thoughtful—deep, meditative thinkers.
  • Carefully considers opinions—grounded in clarity and moral awareness.
  • Excellent at defining, diagnosing, and analyzing complex problems.
  • Strong memory and a desire to learn from past mistakes.
  • Thinks practically—prefers ideas with real-world application.
Challenges
  • May inhibit creative thinking—both in self and others.
  • Can become rigid or overly focused on being “correct.”
  • Slow to adapt to new ideas if they challenge existing beliefs.
Supportive Strategies
  • Balance structure with curiosity—make space for imaginative thought.
  • Invite collaboration and open-ended exploration.
  • Challenge habitual thought patterns through journaling or dialogue.
  • Allow time for reflection, but avoid perfectionism or overanalysis.
